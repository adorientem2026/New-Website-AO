Beyond the War, Hope

In war zones, children lose far more than homes; they lose schooldays, safety, silence, and the right to dream. Yet even amid ruins, hope can still be planted. A book placed in a child’s hands can become a shelter, a voice, a horizon—reminding them that the present darkness is not the end of their story.

Who we are

About Us

Ad Orientem Foundation is a Canadian federally incorporated non-profit organization based in Ottawa, ON, dedicated to creating books for children whose lives have been affected by war.

We believe that every child deserves access to stories, knowledge, imagination, and hope, even in the most difficult circumstances. Through the production, publication, and distribution of charitable children’s books, we seek to bring comfort, learning, and inspiration to young readers overseas in war zones.

Our books include both fiction and non-fiction titles, carefully developed for three age groups:

  • Sprouts Talesfor children ages 0–5
  • Page Palsfor children ages 6–10
  • Trailblazers Readsfor children ages 11–15

From gentle stories for early childhood to engaging educational books for older readers, our publications are designed to meet children where they are: emotionally, developmentally, and intellectually.

At Ad Orientem Foundation, books are more than printed pages. They are companions, teachers, windows, and bridges. They can help a child feel seen, spark curiosity, preserve dignity, offer moments of peace amid uncertainty. Above all, they can give hope, and open a path toward a brighter future beyond the hardship of the present.

Our purpose

Our Mission

Our mission is to produce, publish, and distribute charitable children’s books for children overseas in war zones.

Through fiction and non-fiction books created for children ages 0–15, Ad Orientem Foundation seeks to support young readers with stories that comfort, knowledge that empowers, ideas that awaken curiosity, and horizons that inspire hope.

We serve three age groups through dedicated publishing categories:

Sprouts Tales

introduces the youngest children to warmth, rhythm, images, and simple stories.

Page Pals

helps growing readers explore imagination, friendship, discovery, and learning.

Trailblazers Reads

offers older children meaningful fiction and non-fiction that encourages reflection, resilience, and curiosity.

In every book we publish, our purpose is the same: to bring the gift of reading to children facing the hardship of war, and to affirm that their minds, dreams, and futures matter.
